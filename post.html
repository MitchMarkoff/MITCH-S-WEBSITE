The Waterfall Prayer

Why I Trust the Spirit Even When I'm Soaking Wet

Posted by Mitch Markoff on April 10, 2025

Some people find peace in a church pew. I found it at the top of a roaring waterfall in a kayak with my cousin. We’d scouted the rapid for hours — the current was mean, the drop unforgiving. It was the kind of waterfall you don’t just “try out.” Either you send it with confidence, or you don’t send it at all.

Before dropping in, my cousin looked at me and said, “Let’s pray.” There we were, two dudes in helmets and life vests, praying for protection on a river that doesn’t forgive hesitation. And I swear, in that moment, the nerves settled. We felt directed, calm, and somehow... prepared.

We launched. My boat hit the lip of the drop, and the world went silent. For a few seconds, I was flying. Then came the slam of landing — perfect, clean, and upright. We both made it. No swims, no carnage. Just huge grins and adrenaline shaking our hands as we high-fived on the eddy below.

That moment taught me something deeper than paddling technique. It reminded me that God doesn’t just care about Sundays — He’s there in the chaos, in the risk, in the things that make you feel most alive. And when you trust the Spirit, even in a kayak, you’ll land where you’re supposed to.
